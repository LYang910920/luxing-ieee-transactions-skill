# Journal Overlays

These are research-emphasis overlays, not cached submission instructions. Before submission, check the current official journal site and record the retrieval date in `PROJECT_CONFIG.yaml`.

## TIFS

Lead with the adversary, security consequence and defender decision. Define attacker capabilities, information, lateral movement/propagation mechanism and resource constraints. Evaluation should include security-specific metrics, credible baselines, attacker/defender sensitivity and failure boundaries. A control/game formulation must remain visibly tied to security, not appear as an abstract optimization exercise.

## TDSC

Emphasize dependable/secure system behavior, system and failure models, guarantee scope, overhead and robustness. Formal results should identify exactly which faults, attacks or uncertainty are covered. System-level validation or a convincing implementation path strengthens the paper.

## TSMC

Emphasize systems integration, interaction among components, stability/feasibility and system-level interpretation. Explain state and parameter meaning carefully. Make the relationship between mathematical dynamics and system operation explicit.

## TNSE

Justify the network representation—aggregate, degree-based, node-level, graph, temporal or multilayer. Analyze topology, heterogeneity and scale. Use real and synthetic networks where appropriate, and explain whether results depend on network statistics or individual graph instances.

## TCSS

Emphasize computational social mechanisms, awareness/opinion/behavior assumptions, topology, incentives and social or economic interpretation. State ethical and behavioral limitations. Avoid claiming human behavior is fully captured by a stylized dynamical state.

## Cross-journal overlay rule

A paper can use the Lu-Xing Yang macro-architecture in all five venues, but the central evidence must match the journal's center of gravity. Do not submit the same narrative with only the journal name changed.
